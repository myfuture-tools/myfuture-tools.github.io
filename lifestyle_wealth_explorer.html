How these numbers work (assumptions) · tap to expand

Lifestyle choices are an annual spend, redirected and compounded at your chosen return over your chosen horizon. NZ-typical averages:

Lifestyle lever~NZ avg / yr
Overseas family holiday$10,000
Running a newer car$9,000
Higher day-to-day lifestyle$6,000
Private schooling$18,000

Wealth choices are a starting amount that compounds up at a historical long-run rate over the horizon: additional property ~6% (REINZ 30yr), shares/managed fund ~8% (NZX 50 long-run), starting sooner ~8%.

Paid with bad debt? If a lifestyle item is funded by consumer debt, you also pay interest - at NZ's average credit-card rate of ~19.7% p.a. stacked on top. Same money, working against you twice.

An illustrative tool showing how the levers behave - not a forecast of your situation. Sources: Stats NZ, AA / Consumer NZ, RBNZ (19.7%), REINZ (~6%), NZX 50 (~8%), MyFuture guide (7%).
